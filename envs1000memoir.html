Your ENVS 1000 Fictional Flash Memoir 

First read these works of climate flash fiction. Read them carefully and perhaps more than once - they aren't even a page long!

"What Are the Flavours We'll Lose" - a moment in daily life 

 "They Were the Years of Fat Water" -  written looking back from a different climate present 

"From Our Bones Will Blossom Coral Kingdoms" - another daily life moment  

First Time at Waffle House, as a Hurricane Approaches - still another moment in time 

"Waters are Rising" - flash fiction with supernatural elements.  

You can see why this genre is called "flash"! All of these pieces are very short, but they also evoke charcters and a whole world. All of these are set in a climate future, aren't they?

For this assignment you are to write a piece of cliamte flash fiction, like these stories, but I want you to situate yourself in the story! So it is a kind of flash memoir.

The idea for this flash memoir is for you to explore your future -- your career, life, friends, family, living situation, activities -- to consider ways you might be inside of and addressing the climate emergency say 20 years from now - 2046? Or looking backward from 2046 at a revealing moment of climate related action in your life in some prior year. 

For the sake of this assignment your memoir is a personal, speculative fiction set in the future (or maybe set in the future considering a moment in the past). Closely related to "microfiction" and "flash fiction," a flash memoir can be quite short, even a few words or sentences. For this assignment consider the length of a flash memoir to be at least 500 words.

I ask you to compose this memoir in the light of all you have learned this semester in ENVS 1000. I hope that the real you, your personality, values, interest, life plans and aspirations is clearly present in your flash memoir.

The flash memoir is only capturing a carefully imagined and detailed moment, but that moment can and should reveal such things as: how climate change is impacting your world, how the career you chose, how you acted or are acting as a citizen, how you collaborated with friends or family, or how you resisted dominant practices or groups - in short how you are/were/could be involved in addressing the climate emergency.

I can't wait to hear what you think about cliamte literature, the cli-fi I have introduced you to, these flash fictions, and what you learn from writing your own cli-fi future flash memoir!

Some more on flash memoirs:

Flash memoir is a concise, powerful form of creative nonfiction that captures a single, significant moment or memory, using brevity to reveal larger dimensions of your life, much like a snapshot or a "flash" of insight. It focuses on intense, specific details and emotional resonance rather than exhaustive storytelling, making every word count to create a impact and connect readers to a fuller understanding of yourself, values, and activities. 

(In psychology this kind of work is called "episodic future thinking" which some psychology researchers have even tied with increasing motivation to address climate change.)

Online sources to learn more: A Guide to the Micro-Memoir, Flash Memoir Award Winners (includes 4 examples), Chicago Land Writing Center (includes links to examples.
